Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE) is a variant of Bluetooth that was introduced
in Bluetooth 4.0

Bluetooth versions 1.0 to 4.2 are collectively referred to as "Bluetooth
Classic." These versions use a combination of frequency hopping spread
spectrum (FHSS) and adaptive frequency hopping (AFH) to avoid interference

 

Bluetooth version 5.0: longer range , higher data rates and better security, introduced BLE

 

Bluetooth version 5.1 introduced several improvements to location services

 

Bluetooth version 5.2, the most recent version of Bluetooth, introduced several new features
