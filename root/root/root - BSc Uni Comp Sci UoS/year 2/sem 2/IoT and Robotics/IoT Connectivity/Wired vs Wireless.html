Wired vs Wireless

wired = electrical signals (or light)

  • twisted pair (UTP) , sheilded twisted pair (STP)

wireless = radio frequency ot optical signals

  • Coaxil cable , fibre optic

 

 

WIRED:

The electromagnetic energy that is sent over a cable attenuates with distance
due to the impedance of the cable and impedance mismatches from different
components connected to the cable

Attenuation (dB) = 20 * log10 (Input (w) / outputs (w) )

 

WIRELESS:

the carrier waveform is launched as an
electromagnetic wave into “free space” from the transmitting device to the
receiving device.

The rate at which it falls is proportional to the inverse of the square of the
distance. 

signal = k / d^2
Where:
k = constant
d = distance from the transmitter
