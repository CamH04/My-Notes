SHA

The Secure Hash Algorithms
 

SHA uses a series of bitwise operations, modular additions, and compression functions to process the input data and generate the hash

 

SHA1, SHA2 and SHA3:
SHA1 discontinued after 2010 – 160bits
SHA2 developed by the NSA in 2001 – Still in use in things like TLS and SSL
224, 256, 384, or 512 bits
SHA3 published in 2016 after attacks against MD5 and SHA1 were shown (Same lengths as SHA2)
